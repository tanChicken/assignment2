MALAYSIA TOURISM STATISTICS (2016-2023)

In recent years, Malaysia's tourism sector has experienced remarkable growth, driven by its variety of attractions, rich cultural heritage, and strategic location. To better understand the industry's performace and its overall impact during this period, it is crucial to anlyze key performance indicators.

Tourist Arrivals To Malaysia By Country (45 Countries)

From 2016 to 2023, Singapore was the top country contributing to tourist arrivals with over 61 million visitors, followed by Indonesia, Thailand, China and Brunei.

Tourist Arrivals and Receipts

Malaysia's tourist arrivals, peaking between 2016 and 2019, with totals exceeding 25 millions tourists annually. However, the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 and 2021 led to a dramatic decline in tourist arrivals and receipts.

Despite the challenges, Malaysia implemented various recovery measures to revitalize its tourism industry, including vaccination campaigns. Following the relaxation of global travel restrictions in 2022, Malaysia witnessed a significant rebound in tourism, and the positive trend continued into 2023 showing the Malaysian resilience of the Malaysia tourism industry

Expenditure Components

Shopping consistently remained the largest expenditure category, peaking in 2019 at RM28,944.2 (33.6% of total expenditure). It saw a dip in 2021 due to the pandemic. Many retail outlets, malls, and tourist shopping destinations were either closed or operating with restrictions, reducing tourist spending on shopping. During pandemic, tourists prioritized essential services like accommodation and food over shopping, thus, accomodation and food & beverages expenditures increased in ranking during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Hotel Guests By States

In 2023, Kuala Lumpur, the capital city of Malaysia, leads with 18.4 million arrivals due to its status as a business and tourist center, drawing visitors for its iconic landmarks, shopping and international events.

Pulau Pinang with over 7.2 million in 2023 arrivals is also a major tourism hub due to its cultural heritage, beaches and UNESCO-listed George Town. This makes it a popular destination despite having a smaller area compared to other states.

Pahang as a notable number of hotel guest arrivals despites its lower arrival per KM2 due to its vast land area. The lower density of arrivals per KM2 doesn't imply a lack of tourism but rather that the arrivals are spread across its extensive area. Pahang's appeal lies in its famous attractions like Cameron Highlands, Taman Negara, and Genting Highlands.

Mode Of Transport

In 2023, land transportation was the dominant mode, accounting for 55.8% of arrivals, with over 11 million tourists using this method. This reflects the convenience and accessibility of land routes, especially for regional travelers. Air travel followed with 36.7% of arrivals, highlighting the importance of international and long-distance travel for tourism, with over 7.3 million tourists arriving by air.

Meanwhile, sea travel contributed 5.6%, likely linked to coastal destinations or cruise tourism. Rail travel had the smallest share at 1.9%, indicating limited infrastructure or less preference for rail journeys among tourists. The contrast between these modes shows the diverse ways tourists access destinations, with a strong preference for road and air travel over other forms of transport.
